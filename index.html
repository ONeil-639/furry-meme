Engineers are creative problem solvers who innovate solutions that shape our future!.

Engineering is a broad field consisting of 9 main disciplines as explained below.

Around the world, engineers of all disciplines work together in order to help make people and communities healthy, happy and safe.



Aerospace Engineering


Aerospace engineering spans many disciplines, but is generally broken down into a few subfields:
astrodynamics, flight structures and materials, propultion and fluid dynamics, instrumentation, control systems and navigations.


Source: teachengineering.org.


Biomedical Engineering


Biomedical engineering spans many disciplines, but it is generally broken down into a few subareas:
math, physics, chemistry, design, human biology, anatomy, physiology, biomechanics, neuroscience and bioethics.


Source: teachengineering.org.


Civil Engineering


Civil engineering spans many disciplines, but it is generally broken down into the following subfields:
built environment, architectural, construction, geotechnical and earthquakes, transpotation and water resources.


Source: teachengineering.org.

Environmental Engineering


Environmental engineering spans many disciplines, but it is generally broken down into a few subareas:
math, physics, chemistry, along with understanding of environmental science such as biology, water chemistry, hydrology and atmospheric science.


Source: teachengineering.org.

Chemical & Biological Engineering


Chemical and biological engineering spans many disciplines, but is generally broken into the following subfields:
chemistry, chemical processes, petrochemical engineering, nanotechnology, plastics, materials engineering biology, biochemical engineering, environmental health engineering, bioinformatics.


Source: teachengineering.org.

Mechanical Engineering


Mechanical engineering also span many disciplines. However, it is broken down into the following subfields:
properties of materials, solid and fluid mechanics, thermodynamics, heat transfer, control, instrumentation, design, and manufacturing of mechanical systems.


Source: teachengineering.org.

Agricultural Engineering


Agricultural engineering spans many disciplines, but is generally broken down into the following subareas:
soil science, plant biology, organic chemistry, climatology and atmospheric science, along with engineering practices around equipment design, molecular optimization, and waste management.


Source: teachengineering.org.

Electrical Engineering


Electrical engineering spans many disciplines, but it is generally broken down into a few subareas:
energy systems, power engineering, microelectronics, systems and control, telecommunications and signal processing, digital and analog electronics.


Source: teachengineering.org.

Software Engineering


Software engineering spans many disciplines, but it is generally broken down into a few subareas:
mathematical foundations, algorithms, data structures, artificial intelligence, communication and security, databases, software engineering, and programming language.


Source: teachengineering.org.
